VAX-IN RESEARCH FACILITY

SUBJECT INTEGRATION REPORT: B-CLASS DESIGNATION (INTERIM)

Details:

Date: 06-15-93
Researchers:
Classification: Classified

Objective:

Document infection progression in selected subjects under controlled containment conditions and evaluate potential for behavioral stabilization.

Summary of Findings:

  • Selected subjects exhibit standard infection progression consistent with established Z-class response model.
  • In isolated cases, irregular behavioral interruptions have been recorded during stimulus exposure events.
  • These interruptions present as brief pauses in aggressive response cycles, followed by return to baseline behavior.
  • No stable suppression state has been achieved under current containment variables.
  • Data anomalies have increased in frequency during later-stage containment cycles.
  • Subject variability appears higher than predicted under existing infection models.

Notes / Caveats:

  • Irregular behavioral events are currently classified as non-critical anomalies pending replication.
  • System monitoring logs indicate intermittent data inconsistencies during active trials.
  • External interference cannot be confirmed but is not ruled out due to repeated signal disruption patterns.
