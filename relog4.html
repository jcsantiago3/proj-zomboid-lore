SUBJECT ANOMALY REPORT: B-001 DESIGNATION (BOB)

Details:

Date: 07-07-93
Researchers:
Classification: Classified

Objective:

Evaluate abnormal behavioral deviations observed in Subject B-001 (Bob) under standard infection conditions and determine viability as a replicable containment outcome.

Summary of Findings:

  • Bob follows standard Z-class response behavior during initial exposure phases.
  • Unlike control samples, subject exhibits repeated interruptions in aggressive pursuit cycles when exposed to specific external stimuli.
  • Recorded instances show brief pauses in hostility when encountering familiar auditory and visual cues.
  • Bob does not fully transition into suppression state but does not maintain full baseline aggression stability either.
  • Behavioral response patterns cannot be fully classified under existing failure models.
  • Bob demonstrates the closest observed deviation from baseline infection behavior across all test groups.

Notes / Caveats:

  • Bob is currently classified as the first viable anomalous stability case.
  • This behavior has not been successfully replicated in other subjects under identical conditions.
  • Further study required to determine if deviation is environmental, biological, or externally influenced.
  • Replication protocols have been authorized despite incomplete understanding of observed behavior.
  • Bob is ready for the ALZ-113 Serum.
