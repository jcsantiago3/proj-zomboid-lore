BOB WALKER — FINAL DIARY ENTRY 03

CONTAINMENT TRANSFER

I’ve been moved again. Everything past this point feels less like recovery and more like structured isolation. Rooms are sealed. Movement is controlled. Conversations are monitored or cut short when they go too far.

They still insist this is part of the cure research program — that what I’m undergoing is advanced stabilization work for exposed individuals. I try to hold onto that explanation because it’s the only one that still connects everything together.

But what keeps me steady isn’t what they tell me. It’s what I remember from outside.

Johnny drew a picture of me once. I didn’t think much of it at the time. Just a child’s drawing — simple lines, exaggerated shapes, the usual things kids do when they try to make sense of adults.

But he wrote something under it.

“Dad will save the world.”

I keep coming back to that sentence more than anything else. Not as hope. Not as comfort. As something I’m supposed to become.

Because if I’m here — if I agreed to this place, if I let myself be separated from them — then it can’t just be for nothing.

I tell myself this is still part of the cure research. That what they’re doing here is leading somewhere. That being moved deeper means I’m closer to helping fix what’s outside.

If Johnny believes I can save the world, then I have to believe I’m still capable of becoming that person. Even if I don’t fully understand what this place is turning me into.

- Bob
