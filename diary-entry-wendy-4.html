WENDY WALKER — FINAL DIARY ENTRY 04

WAREHOUSE INCIDENT

Bob was moved into the warehouse tonight. I waited for the guard rotation before going in.

I entered through a second-floor window and made my way down inside.

I only intended to see him and leave.

He was there when I reached the lower level.

At first I stopped moving. I thought I had made a mistake.

But it didn’t change when I got closer.

He was standing. He was moving. But nothing about it felt like him anymore.

I said his name.

No response. No recognition. Nothing that told me he was still there.

Carl gave me a syringe earlier and told me it would help when I saw Bob. I used it because I believed him.

I don’t know what it was meant to do. But it changed something in him. Not into something better. Into something unstable. Like whatever was holding him together stopped holding at all.

That’s when he bit me.

There was no hesitation. No recognition. No response when I looked at him.

It was just movement reacting the moment I was close enough.

I pushed him away and grabbed a bat nearby just to get distance.

I swung because I didn’t know how else to stop myself from staying there.

I got out of the warehouse after that.

I believe I can still think clearly enough to write this, but it feels like it won’t stay that way for long.

Bob is still inside that place. But I don’t think the person I knew is inside it anymore.

I keep trying to hold onto his name when I think about him. It doesn’t feel like it belongs to what I saw.

I'm sorry, Bob.

I'm sorry, Johnny.

- Wendy
