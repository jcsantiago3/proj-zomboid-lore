HIDDEN ITEM

I told myself I was only moving supplies. That was easier than admitting what I was really carrying.

Just outside the housing gate, the trees start swallowing everything. The grass grows too high out there. Feels like the woods are trying to hide the world from itself.

There’s a stupid little garden gnome near the edge of the fence line. I stopped beside it for a while. Don’t know why. Maybe because it was the last thing out there that looked normal.

After that, I headed south.

Branches kept catching on the box while I dragged it through the dirt. I almost left it there more than once. The deeper I went, the quieter everything became.

Found an old bird bath half-filled with rainwater and leaves. We used to joke about buying one someday.

Dumb thing to remember at a time like this. That’s where I finally had to rest. The box was getting too heavy to drag.

I pulled the box west from there. Not too far. Just far enough that nobody wandering through the woods would stumble onto it by accident.

I still remember the sound the lid made when I closed it.

I still remember what they looked like before all of this happened.

I’m sorry.

I really thought I could keep you safe.

If someone finds this note someday… then I guess you found them too.

Please don’t open the box unless you absolutely have to.

I'm really sorry. . . Applebeigh.

(paper torn)
