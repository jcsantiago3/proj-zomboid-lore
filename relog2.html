VAX-IN RESEARCH FACILITY

CONTAINMENT OBJECTIVE REPORT: SUPPRESSION MODEL INITIATIVE

Details:

Date: 07-14-93
Researchers:
Classification: Classified

Objective:

Define and evaluate methods for reducing infected hostility in order to support improved containment control outcomes.

Summary of Findings:

  • Early suppression attempts show temporary reduction in aggressive behavior under controlled conditions.
  • Subjects return to baseline hostile response patterns once external stimuli are reintroduced.
  • Partial calming states are unstable and cannot be maintained beyond short observation windows.
  • Adjusted containment variables show no consistent long-term impact on pursuit behavior.
  • Behavioral suppression attempts appear to accelerate overall system instability in some cases.
  • No repeatable method has been identified to produce a stable non-hostile infected state.

Notes / Caveats:

  • Current suppression models are considered incomplete and not reliable for field application.
  • Behavioral instability increases with repeated intervention attempts.
  • All tested protocols fail to produce sustained deviation from baseline infection behavior.
