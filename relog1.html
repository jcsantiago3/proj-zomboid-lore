VAX-IN RESEARCH FACILITY

INFECTED BEHAVIORAL BASELINE REPORT: Z-CLASS RESPONSE MODEL v1.0

Details:

Date: 07-10-93
Researchers:
Classification: Classified

Objective:

Define how infected subjects behave to support containment and control procedures.

Summary of Findings:

  • Infected subjects consistently show immediate aggression toward living targets within range.
  • Primary triggers include movement, sound, and direct visual contact.
  • Behavior is stimulus-driven with no signs of planning, hesitation, or adaptive decision-making.
  • Subjects continue pursuing targets even after loss of sight, following last known direction.
  • Multiple infected individuals do not coordinate but will independently converge on the same stimulus.
  • Physical barriers may delay movement but do not stop pursuit behavior.

Notes / Caveats:

  • Behavior pattern is consistent across all tested subjects regardless of infection duration or origin.
  • No evidence of retained identity or cognitive recognition under baseline conditions.
  • Only confirmed method of stopping subject activity is physical incapacitation.
  • Current behavior model is considered stable and repeatable for containment use.
