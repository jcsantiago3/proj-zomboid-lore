BOB WALKER — PERSONAL LOG

ENTRY 01 — VOLUNTEERING

I signed the papers today. It didn’t feel like a dramatic moment, even though I keep replaying it in my head like it should be one. Just a pen, a table, and someone explaining things in a calm voice that sounded practiced — like they’ve done this conversation many times before.

They told me the facility is working toward a cure or prevention for the infection. Not just treatment, but something more permanent. Something that could actually change the direction of things. I don’t fully understand the science behind it, but I understand what it means if it works.

Wendy was hesitant. I could tell even when she didn’t say it directly. She kept asking questions like “what exactly will you be doing?” and “why you specifically?” I didn’t have answers that satisfied her, only answers that sounded reasonable enough for me to keep going.

They offered relocation for my family — a secure survivor camp under military protection. That part mattered more than I expected. Not because I distrust the outside, but because I’ve seen enough to know that safety is becoming something you don’t get to choose anymore.

I told myself this is responsibility. If there’s a chance to contribute to something that could protect people, then refusing feels like giving up before trying.

I didn’t tell Wendy everything. Not because I wanted to hide it… but because I wasn’t sure I would be able to explain it without sounding like I already made my decision.

I think I already did.

- Bob
